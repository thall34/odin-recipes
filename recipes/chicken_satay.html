Chicken Satay
Description
This Chicken Satay recipe was one of the first Indian recipes my wife and I tried and fell in love with. It can be served on skewers, but we prefer serving it over top of rice.
This recipe is extra messy, I recommend using glass cookware and containers to avoid staining. This recipe serves 2-4 people.
Ingredients
  • 8 Boneless, Skinless Chicken Thighs or 2 large Boneless, Skinless Chicken Breasts
  • 3/4 cup Creamy Peanut Butter
  • 3/4 cup Coconut Milk
  • 3 cloves Garlic, Peeled
  • 2 tsp Minced Ginger
  • 2 tsp Sriracha
  • 1 tsp Curry Powder
  • 1 tsp Cumin
  • 1 tsp Turmeric
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 2 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Lime Juice
  • Chopped Peanuts, for Garnish
  1. Add peanut butter, coconut milk, garlic, ginger, sriracha, curry powder, cumin, turmeric, salt, soy sauce, and lime juice into food processor.
  2. Blend the ingredients in the processor until smooth.
  3. Cut chicken into small 1-2 inch cubes, add to the full bowl of marinade if serving on rice. Reserve half the marinade in a container before adding chicken if serving on skewers.
  4. Let the chicken marinate for at least 2hrs.
  5. Preheat oven to 350 degrees fahrenheit.
  6. If serving on rice, put chicken and marinade into an oven safe glass bowl and cook until chicken is cooked through to a minimum internal temperature of 165 degrees fahrenheit.
  7. Alternatively, if serving on skewers remove chicken pieces from marinade and shake off excess one at a time and then skewer. Cook to the same minimum internal temperature as step 6
  8. Remove from oven, if serving on rice use a deep spoon to add chicken and marinade overtop of the rice, if serving on skewers, remove and wait for chicken to cool for a few mins.
  9. Garnish with chopped peanuts.
