Onion Bhaji

Home


Description

This Onion Bhaji recipe is one of my favourite recipes to have as a side for Indian cuisine. It could also be described as a spiced onion fritter... Kind of like an onion ring, but way better!

A perfect pairing for my Chicken Satay recipe, this makes about 10-12 bhajis

Ingredients

  • Vegetable Oil for Frying
  • 2 Large Onions, Peeled and Thinly Sliced
  • 1 tbsp Minced Ginger
  • 1 Green Chili, Finely Chopped
  • 1 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 tsp Ground Coriander
  • 1 tsp Chili Flakes
  • 3/4 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Garlic Salt
  • 1 1/2 cups + 2 tbsp Gram Flour
  • 1 tbsp Cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1/2 cup + 1 tbsp Water
  1. Heat oil in large skillet over medium heat, oil should be about an inch deep, but not more than half the pan.
  2. Add to a bowl the ginger, green chili, cumin, coriander, chili flakes, salt, garlic salt, gram flour, cornstarch, and baking soda.
  3. Mix the dry ingredients, then add the water and mix until you get a thick batter.
  4. Add sliced onion to bowl and mix to coat the onion thoroughly.
  5. Carefully add a spoon shaped ball of the onion mixture into the oil, shape it into a 2-3 inch flat fritter shape.
  6. Repeat until you have the skillet full of bhajis frying at the same time.
  7. Fry on each side until the coating is golden brown.
  8. Remove from pan with slotted spoon and drain oil with a strainer or on an oil drain rack.
  9. Repeat steps 5-8 until the mixture has been used up, you can keep cooked bhajis warm in an oven set to 250 degrees fahrenheit.
  10. Serve alongside other Indian cuisine or serve with sweet chili sauce.
